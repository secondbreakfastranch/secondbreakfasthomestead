### Pillar 1 — Livestock

**Pasture-Raised and Forest-Fed**

We raise pigs on rotated pasture and forest understory, not concrete. The
difference shows up in the meat: better marbling, cleaner fat, and an animal
that lived the way it was meant to.

Laying hens follow the pigs, scratching through soiled bedding and turning it
into rich compost. The eggs speak for themselves — deep orange yolks, firm
whites, and flavor that factory farms can't fake.

[Browse Livestock →] — Pastured pork, eggs, meat & processing

---

### Pillar 2 — Plants

**Started Right, Grown in Place**

Every plant on this homestead starts in living soil. We grow starters, fruit
trees, pond plants, and soil-building perennials like comfrey — all selected
for our zone and climate. No growth regulators, no synthetic shortcuts.

Plants that leave here are ready for real dirt, real sun, and real weather.
They don't need to be babied — they need to be planted.

[Browse Plants →] — Starters, trees, pond plants, comfrey

### Pillar 3 — Aquatics

**Living Water Systems**

A pond is not a decoration — it's an ecosystem. We breed ornamental fish
(medaka, celestial pearl danios, guppies) in outdoor tubs and lined ponds,
balanced with plants and bacteria — not chemical additives.

The same approach applies to aquascaping and pond supply. Start with the
biology, then add the aesthetics.

### Values Block

**How We Work**

- **Regenerative Practice** — Every output is also an input. Manure feeds
  soil, soil feeds plants, plants clean water, water grows fish. No waste, no
  shortcuts.
- **Integrated Systems** — Livestock, plants, and aquatics are not separate
  departments. They're one system. We design them that way.
- **Practical Stewardship** — We share what works and admit what doesn't.
  No marketing language. Just methods you can replicate on your own land,
  whether it's five acres or a backyard.
- **Family-Centered** — This operation is built for the long haul. Decisions
  are made with the next generation in view, not the next quarter.
- **Transparent & Honest** — If something failed, we'll say so. If we're
  still learning, you'll know. Every claim on this site is backed by
  something we've actually done.

## Tone Checklist (for all future copy)

- Direct, not folksy
- Professional but warm
- Specific claims only (numbers, methods, results)
- No "y'all," no "down on the farm," no forced charm
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Respect the reader's intelligence — assume they know things
